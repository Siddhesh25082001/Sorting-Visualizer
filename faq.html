Frequently Asked Questions

What is Fake News?
Fake news, also known as junk news, pseudo-news, alternative facts, false news or hoax news, is a form of news consisting of deliberate disinformation or hoaxes spread via traditional news media (print and broadcast) or online social media
How does our Project Work?
The Entire Process revolves around two Machine learning models which work at the backend of the website. On entering the url of the desired news to be detected, the article or the news is extracted from that particular site. Then, the article is being predicted as a real article or a fake one based on the classification methods of the model. Coupled with this, an API also works at the backend itsell to sparse keywords from the articles and searches for some relative articles via an API called ‘Event Registry’ that searches related articles based on the keywords extracted.
Then, the process shifts to the second Machine Learning model that checks the similarity between the extracted article and the articles given by Event Registry. Based on the similarity percentage, if it exceeds the required claim percentage of the article to be true, then it is declared as a Real article or a news. Thus, through the consensus of both the Machine learning models, the decision is executed.
